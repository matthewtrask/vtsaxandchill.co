What is VTSAX?

VTSAX is a Vanguard Mutual Fund you can invest in. To back up. a mutual fund is a fund managed by a broker that pools people's money together. So by buying into VTSAX you join thousands of others doing the same thing.

VTSAX is compromised of the entire stock market. That includes Tesla, Apple, Amazon, Google, Delta, and every other offering in the American Stock Markets.

Instead of trying to bet big on one of those companies above, you are betting on all of them. As JL Collins puts it (paraphrasing) "There will always be winners, and there will always be losers. Instead of trying to pick a winner, or avoid a loser, you let the market do it for you".

Is it really that simple?

Yes. This doesn't mean you can't play the market, but if you want a solid retirement nest egg, putting as much as you can into VTSAX will be a win.

It currently sits at an 18% YTD return. So if you put $10,000 in, it will make you a cool $1,800 in a year. Keep adding those numbers year after year and you can see how it wins.

How to get started

Like this whole thing has stated its easy.

Step 1: Open an account at Vanguard.

Step 2: Move $3,000 into your account.

Step 3: Buy VTSAX

Step infinity: after the initial $3,000 investment, you can add money freely. Be it $10, $100, or more. Just keep adding to your account. Keep it as simple as that.

What if you can't buy it now?

Like this whole thing has stated its easy.

Step 1: Open an account at Vanguard.

Step 2: Move however much you can.

Step 3: Buy VTI

Step infinity: bid your time, putting in money when you can. VTI is an ETF (Electronically Traded Fund) and a mirror image of VTSAX. The difference is that you can automate investments into VTSAX, but not VTI. When you get to the $3,000 minimum, decide if you want to move to VTSAX or stay with VTI. (I would move).
